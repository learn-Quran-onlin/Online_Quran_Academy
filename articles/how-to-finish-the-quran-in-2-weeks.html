How to Finish the Quran in 2 Weeks

Last Updated On: May 11, 2026

Many people set a goal to complete the Quran in just two weeks, whether during Ramadan, on special occasions, or as a personal spiritual challenge. Achieving this requires a well-structured plan, discipline, and consistency.

A practical schedule should focus on daily reading targets, optimal recitation times such as Fajr and Asr, minimizing distractions, and tracking progress. Using one Mushaf consistently, especially a 16-line Mushaf, can also help you stay organized and maintain a steady pace.

1. Make a Customized Schedule That Prioritizes Quran Reading

A structured schedule is essential for staying on track. Set fixed recitation times that align with your daily routine, such as after Salah, early in the morning, or during quiet periods.

When planning your schedule, consider:

  • The number of hours you can dedicate daily.
  • Quiet times with minimal distractions.
  • Short breaks to maintain focus and avoid fatigue.
  • Temporarily reducing non-essential activities.

A realistic and clear plan helps you stay consistent and makes the goal easier to achieve.

2. Set Clear Daily Reading Targets

To complete the Quran in 14 days, you need to read at least 2 to 3 Juz per day. Breaking the reading into smaller sessions makes the goal more manageable.

  • 2 Juz per day = around 15 days completion.
  • 3 Juz per day = around 10 days completion with extra time for revision or catching up.
  • Reading portions after each Salah helps divide the load across the day.

Dividing your reading prevents exhaustion and helps you stay consistent.

3. Prioritize Recitations in Fajr and Asr Time

The most peaceful and productive times for Quran recitation are often early morning after Fajr and late afternoon around Asr.

During these times, distractions are usually fewer, allowing for focused and meaningful recitation. Allocating a significant portion of your daily reading to these times can improve consistency.

4. Read More Than 2 Juz Each Day When Possible

The Quran consists of 30 Juz, so reading more than 2 Juz daily helps you stay safely within your two-week target. If possible, aim for 3 Juz on some days to create a buffer.

  • Read 1 Juz in the morning.
  • Read 1 Juz in the afternoon.
  • Read another Juz in the evening if possible.

Use weekends or free days to read extra portions and stay ahead of schedule.

5. Use a Quran App for Progress Tracking

A Quran app can help you track your daily reading goals and stay motivated. Apps such as Quran.com, Tanzil, or similar Quran reading tools can help you:

  • Set daily reading goals.
  • Receive reminders.
  • Listen to audio recitations.
  • Mark completed Ayahs, pages, or Juz.

Digital tracking helps you stay organized and consistent throughout the two weeks.

6. Choose a Distraction-Free Environment

A calm and quiet setting is important for maintaining concentration. Distractions can slow you down and reduce the quality of your recitation.

  • Choose a quiet corner at home or recite in a mosque.
  • Turn off phone notifications.
  • Inform family members about your Quran reading time.
  • Keep water nearby and sit comfortably.

A dedicated recitation space improves focus and helps you build a stronger routine.

7. Recite Aloud for Better Focus and Learning

Reciting aloud can help you:

  • Improve concentration.
  • Notice pronunciation mistakes.
  • Strengthen Tajweed and fluency.
  • Engage both hearing and reading together.

If possible, recite in a quiet place where you feel comfortable hearing your own voice.

8. Use a 16-Line Mushaf and Stick to It

Using a 16-line Mushaf is helpful for intensive reading because its structure makes it easier to track progress. Sticking to one Mushaf throughout the journey also improves familiarity with page layouts.

  • Use sticky notes to mark completed sections.
  • Avoid switching between different Mushaf layouts.
  • Keep your Mushaf in an accessible place.

Familiarity with one Mushaf supports smoother reading and better consistency.

How to Finish the Quran in 20 Days?

If finishing in two weeks feels too difficult, a 20-day plan is more flexible and still highly achievable.

  • Read 1.5 Juz per day.
  • Read around 30 pages daily.
  • Read 6 pages after each Salah.
  • Or read 3 pages before and 3 pages after every Salah.

This approach makes completion easier while still keeping you consistent.

Conclusion

Finishing the Quran in a set timeframe requires dedication, discipline, and a structured plan. With clear targets, consistent reading times, reduced distractions, and daily tracking, completing the Quran in two weeks is achievable.
